## Chief of Staff — Challenge

You made the shortlist.

We got a lot of applications. You're one of a handful we want to see do real work before we make a call. So instead of more interview questions, here's a challenge built from things actually on my plate.

Quick context on who you'd be working with:

I'm Max Hertan. I built Megaphone and scaled it past $20M ARR. I co-founded Snowball Agency with Ryan Hashemi, run Alto Studios and Vids, and have a few more ventures in motion — which is exactly why I need a Chief of Staff. There's more good work to do than there is of me to do it.

This role is the right hand seat. You'll move between strategy, marketing, hiring, and operations in the same week. You'll own things end-to-end, not just track them. If you want a calendar-management job, this isn't it. If you want to learn how multiple companies actually get built and have your fingerprints on the outcome, keep reading.

Top 3 submissions get $100 as a thank-you for the time, regardless of how the role shakes out.

Now the work.

## The Setup

Two problems. One marketing build, one hiring search. Both are real — the kind of thing that lands in my inbox on a normal Tuesday. Together they show me how you think, research, and ship.

You have 48 hours.

## Challenge 1 — Build a Funnel for Volley

Volley is an AI + human language coaching app at $99/month. Their current landing page: https://getvolley.ai/lp3

Pretend I just handed you the account. What would you change, and how would you prove it works?

**Deliverables:**

1. **A landing page variant.** Mock-up, wireframe, or actual build — your call. Include the rationale: what you changed, why, and what hypothesis you're testing.
2. **3 Meta ad angles with creative direction.** Copy for each angle plus visual direction or mock-ups. Show range — different audiences, different hooks, different promises.
3. **A 1-page testing plan.** How you'd structure and sequence the tests, what metrics matter, and — this part matters — what you'd kill first if the numbers came in soft.

I'd like to see a beautifully presented Notion or PDF deck. But more importantly, I'm looking for the instincts of someone who can run a test, read the data, and make a call.

## Challenge 2 — Find Snowball a Head of Sales

Snowball Agency (snowball.agency) needs a Head of Sales. Find three people on LinkedIn who could actually do the job.

**Deliverables (for each candidate):**

1. LinkedIn profile link
2. Current role & company
3. 2–3 sentences on why they'd crush it
4. The outreach message you'd send to get them on a call

Don't send me obvious org-chart picks. Show me how you hunt — who you found, why them, and what would make them actually open your DM.

## Challenge 3 — Show Your Work

A 5-minute Loom (max — I'll stop watching at 5:01). Walk me through:

1. How you thought about the Volley landing page and ad angles
2. How you found and evaluated the Snowball candidates
3. How you make decisions about conversion, targeting, and talent

The Loom is where this is won or lost. Decks tell me what you did. Loom tells me how you think. (P.S. Don't read a script!)

## What I'm Grading

Strategic thinking. Speed. Taste. The ability to make a call with incomplete information and explain it cleanly. I don't care if your slides are pretty.

## Logistics

2. Include your Loom link
3. Reply on the same email thread that sent you this
4. 48 hours from receipt

Let's see what you've got!
