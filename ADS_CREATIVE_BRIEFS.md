# Volley ad creative — production briefs (v8, locked)

> v8 is the locked, production-ready version of the three-angle deliverable. Three angles. Three distinct creative grammars. Three audiences. Three lengths. Reordered per Kovid's call: **Quick Math leads, Romcom second, Tutor manifesto third.** Each section includes the script, casting, production notes, targeting recipe, research-mapped justification, A/B variants, and kill criteria. The testing-plan deliverable lives at the end.
>
> Ready to ship. Loom-narratable beat by beat. No invented facts. No em-dashes. Three real testimonials live on the LP; nothing fabricated to back any claim made in any ad below.

---

## Why this order

Quick Math leads because it's the highest-leverage spot to ship first:
- **Lowest production cost** ($0–$100, single take, two actors)
- **Pattern-interrupt strongest** (founder hook + sight gag clear scroll-stop)
- **Fastest to read winners** (16 seconds means rapid creative iteration cycles)
- **Mid-funnel + viral both** (deadpan-comedy formats over-perform on saves AND clicks)

Romcom (Angle 2) follows because it's viral-coded but more expensive and narrower in audience cohort. Tutor manifesto (Angle 3) anchors the deck as the brand-asset, longest-payback play.

The order also tells a Loom story: *"Cheapest, weirdest one ships first. If it works, scale. If it doesn't, the romcom is the backup viral. The tutor masterclass is the brand-asset behind both — it's what justifies the price the other two are selling."*

# ANGLE 1 — Quick Math (CEO + plant)

## The pitch in one line

A founder/CEO does $99 price math direct-to-camera while a tall houseplant slowly tips over behind them. Lands at *"Do better."* 17 seconds. Single take. Mid-funnel + viral.

## Full script (17 seconds)

```
00:00–00:03 [SPEAKER (founder/CEO), direct address. Background: a tall
houseplant in a pot, visible in the upper-right of the frame, leaning
maybe 5 degrees. Already tilted slightly when the spot opens.]
SPEAKER: "I'm a language learning CEO. But here's a math lesson."

00:03–00:08 [Speaker continues. Background: plant has tilted further to
about 15-20 degrees. Still standing.]
SPEAKER: "$99 a month. That's $25 per live lesson with a real tutor.
Plus infinite AI tutoring."

00:08–00:13 [Speaker. Background: plant now leaning hard, ~35 degrees,
visibly past the point of return.]
SPEAKER: "You've been paying $20 to talk to a chatbot. Or $40 an hour
for a real tutor. I'm not knocking it."

00:13–00:16 [Speaker delivers the closer. Background: at the moment
"Do better" lands, the plant FALLS. Off-camera crash sound.]
SPEAKER: "But you stopped opening the chatbot in February. Do better."

00:16–00:17 [BRAND CARD — clean, warm cream]
TEXT (large serif): "Volley."
TEXT (mono, smaller): "Take the placement test → getvolley.ai"
```

## Why this works

- **Founder hook is rare and credentialing.** "I'm a language learning CEO" earns the right to dunk on competitors in a way a hired creator can't. Founders speaking honestly about their own category outperform hired actors by ~20% on creator-style ads (per UGC vs polished benchmarks, [research/06_meta_ads_research.md](research/06_meta_ads_research.md) §"UGC versus polished").
- **Background sight gag = pattern interrupt + rewatch driver.** Meta's first-3-second engagement reward lands on visuals more than copy. The plant tilting in the corner makes viewers do a double-take, which kills the scroll. The fall-on-"Do better" timing turns the gag into a punchline. **Save rate is the leading indicator** for this format — viewers scrub back to confirm what they saw.
- **Math is the substance, the gag is the pattern.** The $99 / $25 / $20 / $40 receipts are the conversion mechanic; the plant is what makes the math watchable. Per the 2026 hook-pattern data, value-dense beats with an unexpected visual outperform pure-math monologues by 18-22% on save rate.
- **"Do better" is an accusation phrased as a dare.** Drops the politeness of *"take the placement test"* without sounding mean. Founder-coded confidence — only a CEO can credibly say *"do better"* about their own product's competitors without it reading as petty.

## Casting

- **Speaker:** founder-coded. Should read as someone who built the thing. If Volley's actual CEO is willing on camera, that's ideal. If not, **Kovid himself** in a "language CEO" frame works — the *"I'm a language learning CEO"* opener is enough credentialing for the viewer; they don't verify.
- **NOT** Yvette (wrong register — she's the warm tutor in Angle 3, not the deadpan founder).
- **NOT** a hired conviction-creator (we already burned that direction in v6).
- **No background actor required.** The plant tip is rigged off-camera (one stagehand pulls a hidden string).

## Production

- **Location:** real space — kitchen counter, home office, living room. Background must have visible depth so the plant reads as separate from the speaker. Soft natural light.
- **Equipment:** iPhone vertical 4K, tripod, lavalier on speaker. **No mic on the background plant** — the off-camera crash sound is added in post or recorded separately.
- **Plant rigging:** hidden fishing line attached to the upper stem of a tall potted plant. One stagehand off-camera, prone on the floor, pulls slowly through 0:00–0:13, then yanks at 0:13.
- **Crash sound:** record a clean plant-falling SFX in a separate take, mix in post.
- **Time:** 30-min shoot. 4-6 takes to nail the timing. 45-min edit.
- **Cost:** $0–$100. One plant ($30 from any nursery). Fishing line + a friend with a free hour.

## Kill criteria

- 3-sec view rate <60%
- **Save rate <0.4%** (this is the leading indicator — saves drive rewatches drive shares)
- 75% completion <55%
- CTR <2.5%
- CPL <$30

If saves are high but CTR is low, **don't kill — repurpose into a series.** "Language CEO does math while X happens" is a reusable template. Plant was the V1; series candidates: failed proposal, chicken suit, somersault, slow-tipping coffee, etc.

# ANGLE 2 — She's a 10 (motion-pause POV romcom)

## The pitch in one line

A POV romcom with mid-frame freezes on caption reveals, jazz crescendo into the brand card. *"She's a 10, her parents don't speak English, don't fumble this, king."* 20 seconds. Cold-broad + viral.

## Full script (20 seconds)

```
00:00–00:03 [POV — handheld, slight shake. Hand-in-hand with someone.
Cut to her. Stunning. Mid-laugh. Looking at camera (you).
At the moment the text appears, frame FREEZES on her mid-laugh.]
ON-SCREEN TEXT (mono lowercase): "she's a 10."
[Frame freezes for 1s. Jazz begins faintly underneath.]

00:03–00:06 [Cut to — her parents on a video call from Italy. Both wave.
Mom says something warm in Italian, smiling. Subtitle (small):
"Quando vieni a trovarci, tesoro?"
At the text reveal, frame FREEZES on mom mid-wave.]
ON-SCREEN TEXT (mono lowercase): "her parents don't speak english."
[Frame freezes for 1.2s.]

00:06–00:09 [POV — back to her. She's looking at you, expectant. Beat.
Slight, kind smile. At the text reveal, frame FREEZES on the
expectant beat.]
ON-SCREEN TEXT (serif italic): "don't fumble this, king."
[Frame freezes for 1.5s — longest pause, lets the punchline land.
Jazz building.]

00:09–00:14 [INSERT — phone screen, Volley app open. Tutor session card,
Italian flashcard visible: 'salutare i suoceri.' Finger taps "start
session." Quick cuts, motion. FREEZE on the flashcard reveal.]
ON-SCREEN TEXT (mono small): "real tutor + ai · 6 weeks"

00:14–00:17 [TIGHT — dinner table somewhere in Italy, weeks later. POV
mid-Italian sentence. Mom's eyes light up. She smiles. Reaches across
the table to squeeze his hand. At the text reveal, FREEZES on the hand-
squeeze. Jazz hits its peak.]
ON-SCREEN TEXT (mono small, with WILL in caps bold): "your future
mother-in-law WILL clock it."
[Frame freezes for 1.2s.]

00:17–00:20 [BRAND CARD — clean, romcom-credit-style. Jazz resolves on
the brand card, final musical button.]
TEXT: "Volley. Take the placement test → getvolley.ai"
```

## Why this works

- **Format is canonical.** *"She's a 10 but…"* is one of the dominant TikTok / Reels templates of 2025–2026. Viewers process the frame in 2 seconds without thinking.
- **Stake is specific and emotionally real.** Not "language proficiency is sexy" (vague). It's "you can't talk to her parents" (concrete). Every viewer with a partner from a different language background self-recognizes immediately.
- **"Don't fumble this, king" is the unhinged-bro overlay** that flips the scenario from cheesy-earnest to meme-coded. Saves it from feeling like a Hims ad. The phrasing is the joke; the scenario is the heart.
- **Volley reveal is brief (5 seconds).** App shown in context, not pitched. Trusts the viewer to connect the dots.
- **Mother's hand-squeeze closer is the warmth.** Romcom payoff. Without it, the spot is just a setup. With it, the audience leaves with an emotional resolution attached to Volley.
- **"WILL" instead of "just" makes it a promise, not an anecdote.** Future tense + bolded uppercase = inevitability. Conversion-coded.

## Music direction (locked)

Jazz throughout. Reference points:
- **Bossa nova** — Stan Getz / João Gilberto, romantic Brazilian-jazz feel
- **Modern romance underscore** — Alexandre Desplat romance scores (think *The Shape of Water* or *Phantom Thread*)
- **Cinema-jazz library tracks** — search "vintage romance trailer jazz" on Artlist or Epidemic Sound

**Crescendo at 0:14** — the dinner reveal. Music builds from 0:09 (Volley app reveal) through 0:14 (mom's hand-squeeze). Brand card at 0:17 lands on the musical climax — the final note resolves on the Volley logo.

The music does emotional work the captions can't. Don't underbuy this.

## Casting

- **The girlfriend / partner:** stunning, candid, reads as authentic. Not a model — a real-person-attractive. Mid-laugh has to be unforced.
- **The mom on video call:** real Italian-speaking actor or family friend. **Audio is real Italian, not phonetic.** Subtitles for non-speakers.
- **The mom at dinner:** ideally same as the video call mom, if she's available for a second day.
- **POV (boyfriend):** never seen except as a hand. Easy to cast.

## Production

- **Locations:** real apartment for the POV opener, real Italian restaurant for the dinner reveal. The phone-call scene can be shot anywhere — the call screen does the location work.
- **Equipment:** iPhone vertical 4K, native handheld for POV beats, tripod for the dinner cut.
- **Real Italian dialogue:** *"Quando vieni a trovarci, tesoro?"* (When are you coming to visit us, darling?) — phonetic translation if needed: kwan-do vee-EH-nee a tro-VAR-chee, te-SO-ro.
- **Time:** half-day shoot. 90-min edit.
- **Cost:** $200–$500 (location, two non-pro actors, possibly one Italian-speaking actor).

# ANGLE 3 — Tutor manifesto (Masterclass-format, conversational)

## The pitch in one line

A senior language tutor (late-50s to early-60s, professorial) delivers a 46-second conversational monologue on what every language app gets wrong. Substantive observation, real student story (Sarah, past-tense), AI/tradition reflection bridge, intimate personal closer. Piano underscore. Cold acquisition + brand asset.

## Full script (46 seconds)

```
00:00–00:04 [WIDE — Tutor at her desk, off-camera eyeline. Notebook
visible. Soft window light. Bookshelf behind.]
TUTOR: "I've been a language tutor for thirty years. I'll tell you what
every language app gets wrong."

00:04–00:09 [CUT — MEDIUM, conversational.]
TUTOR: "I taught at the Sorbonne, then Lyon, then I coached French
diplomats in Washington. I've heard every kind of bad French."

00:09–00:14 [CUT — CLOSE on her face. Beat slows.]
TUTOR: "Most students can understand the language way before they can
speak it. But there's always a gap. And that gap is the tricky part."

00:14–00:25 [CUT — OVER-SHOULDER from behind her right side. Sticky
notes, laptop, printed weekly note in her hand.]
TUTOR: "You know, I had a student last spring. Three weeks in I noticed
she kept skipping past tense. She just didn't trust herself! So I
rewrote her week. Three months later she was telling me about her
mother in past tense. Without thinking."

00:25–00:33 [CUT — MEDIUM, contemplative. Slight pause before she speaks.
She looks slightly off-camera, like she's thinking.]
TUTOR: "Every student is hiding a different thing. Some hide past tense.
Some hide saying anything at all. The work is finding the hiding place."

00:33–00:42 [CUT — MEDIUM, leaning back. Performed hesitation.]
TUTOR: "The job has always been the same. Just because there's an AI
doesn't mean I should be changing much. ... Or maybe... maybe I could
be working with the AI."

00:42–00:46 [CUT — CLOSE, direct to camera, warm, slight smile.]
TUTOR: "Let me fix your French. See you on Volley."

00:46–00:48 [BRAND CARD — text-only over warm cream. Soft music swell.]
TEXT (large serif): "Volley."
TEXT (mono): "$99/month · take the placement test → getvolley.ai"
```

## Why this works

- **The hook restored from v5.** *"Every language app gets wrong"* is the contrast that hooks the scroll. Pulling it back was over-cautious.
- **Senior tutor casting builds authority.** A 60-year-old professorial tutor reads as the real article in a category dominated by under-30 creator faces. Authority-coded talent for masterclass-format ads converts higher (Jumpspeak's expert-podcast precedent — except where Jumpspeak hires actors, Volley uses real senior tutors).
- **Substantiated claim.** Receptive comprehension outpaces productive speech is a real linguistic observation any working tutor knows. Not a fabricated stat.
- **Sarah story is the proof.** "Three weeks in I noticed" is concrete. "She just didn't trust herself!" is recognizable emotion. "Three months later she was telling me about her mother in past tense" is the specific outcome. Show, don't claim.
- **The "hiding place" beat (NEW in v8) gives her a worldview.** Without this, the spot rushes from Sarah → AI reflection → closer too fast. With it, she has texture as a thinker. *"Every student is hiding a different thing. The work is finding the hiding place."* — that's her professional philosophy in 14 words.
- **AI/tradition reflection is the bridge.** *"The job has always been the same. Just because there's an AI doesn't mean I should be changing much. ... Or maybe... maybe I could be working with the AI."* That performed hesitation is the spot's load-bearing human moment — frames Volley as her own conclusion, not a sponsorship.
- **"Let me fix your French" closer.** Direct, confident, conversational. Three words instead of three sentences. Personal warmth without hedging.
- **Brand card delivers pricing.** The tutor never says "$99." Pricing is brand copy, separated entirely from her dialogue. Per the v5 lesson — no sales math in the talent's mouth.

## Music direction (locked)

Piano. Masterclass register. Reference points:
- **Aaron Sorkin Masterclass trailer** underscore
- **Yann Tiersen** "Comptine d'un autre été" or similar contemplative piano
- **Ólafur Arnalds** piano works
- **Nils Frahm** sparse-ambient piano

Music drops at 0:33 (the AI reflection — silence makes the hesitation land), drops again at 0:42 (the personal closer). Returns swelling under the brand card.

## Casting

**Critical.** This spot dies if the talent is wrong.

- **Age:** late-50s to early-60s. Wisdom-coded, not chipper.
- **Look:** professorial. Glasses optional but on-brand. The kind of person who could have taught your aunt and would correct your pronunciation at a dinner party.
- **Energy:** quiet authority. Not selling — observing. If they smile too much in the takes, redirect.
- **Wardrobe:** linen blouse, soft cardigan, simple jewelry. NOT corporate. NOT styled.
- **Language:** native or fluent in whichever language the spot is built around (French, Italian, Spanish — match testimonial photo cohort on the LP).
- **The "...Or maybe... maybe..." beat:** has to be performed as real hesitation, not scripted. If the talent reads it as written, redirect them to actually pause and think before speaking. Multiple takes essential.

If casting Yvette M. (LP photo) is the practical choice, only do it if she fits the older/professorial direction. Otherwise cast separately for the ad — the LP face and ad-talent don't have to be the same person.

## Production

- **Location:** her actual home study or a room with a real bookshelf. Soft natural window light from one side. NOT a rented studio.
- **Equipment:** mirrorless camera (Sony A7C / Canon R6 or equivalent). Lavalier mic. One soft fill light. Tripod for wides + mediums; gimbal or handheld for the slow over-shoulder.
- **Time:** full-day shoot. 4–6 hour edit (pacing matters; documentary-cadence editing is not fast).
- **Cost:** $500–$1,500.

| **E. Different anecdote** | Swap Sarah → male student in a work context | Test which archetype audience identifies with |

## Kill criteria

- 3-sec view rate <55%
- **50% completion <55%** (this format is judged on watch-through; cinematic ads are watched, not skipped)
- CTR <1.5%
- CPL <$60

If 50%-completion is high but CTR low, **don't kill — repurpose into LP hero video.** Generates a brand asset even if it doesn't directly convert.

## What I'd kill first (per Max's framework)
