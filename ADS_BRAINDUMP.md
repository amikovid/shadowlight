# Volley ad angles — wide brain-dump

> Reference doc. Ten deliberately varied angles for Meta paid ads. Range was the brief. They span emotional registers (shame, comedy, romance), audience cohorts (travelers, Duo dropouts, partners-of-natives, premium taste buyers), and formats (TikTok-native POV, talking head, static editorial, screen recording). Not all need to ship. Treat as a menu.
>
> The actual deliverable for Max's challenge is **3 angles with creative direction + 1-page testing plan**. The shortlist + sequencing recommendation is at the bottom (§ "If we shipped 3, we'd ship these"). Everything else is bench depth for the testing plan.

---

## 1. The shame thirst-trap
- **Audience:** Anyone who's "been learning" a language 2+ years
- **Hook:** Public-cringe POV
- **Visual:** TikTok-native, handheld. Person on camera. Caption: *"Tell me one sentence in Spanish."* Long pause. They look away, mumble, *"un café... grande?"* Cut to text overlay: *"You've been learning for 3 years."* Beat. *"This is what nobody tells you."*
- **CTA:** *"Stop being bad in private. Get a coach."*
- **Why:** Names the shame the buyer is already feeling. Permission to admit it.

## 2. The trip countdown (paid retargeting)
- **Audience:** Adults who recently booked international flights (Meta interest: "international travel")
- **Hook:** Practical urgency
- **Visual:** Static, editorial. Map of Lisbon / Tokyo / Barcelona with a date overlay: *"94 days."* Subhead: *"You said you'd start months ago. Volley pairs you with a tutor in 2 minutes."*
- **CTA:** *"Get matched before Tuesday."*
- **Why:** Hits the "I have a real reason and I keep procrastinating" buyer at the moment guilt peaks.

## 3. The tutor introduction (60-sec talking head)
- **Audience:** Trust-skeptics, the chatbot-tired
- **Hook:** Proof of human
- **Visual:** Real Volley tutor (whoever's available), shot warm, talking-head. Says: *"Hi, I'm [tutor name]. I'll be your coach. I read your week every Sunday and rewrite next week's plan based on what you're avoiding. The same way I would if you were paying me $80/hour. Except you're not."*
- **CTA:** *"Meet your tutor. Take the placement test."*
- **Why:** Counter-positions every pure-AI competitor with a single piece of footage they can't replicate.

## 4. The Duolingo roast
- **Audience:** Duolingo guilt-pile scrollers
- **Hook:** Comedy / relatable
- **Visual:** Phone notification mock. Duolingo owl: *"YOU LOST YOUR STREAK. ARE YOU OKAY?"* Cut. Text overlay: *"The owl noticed the streak. It didn't notice you stopped speaking the language."* Cut to Volley. *"A real person notices. Then they message you."*
- **CTA:** *"Get coached, not guilt-tripped."*
- **Why:** The owl is meme territory. Volley becomes the grown-up alternative without saying "we're for adults."

## 5. The data flex (honest stat)
- **Audience:** Analytical buyers, ex-Duo / ex-Babbel users
- **Hook:** Blunt fact
- **Visual:** Big editorial type, no animation. *"1 in 4 people quit a language app in the first 30 days. We built Volley for the other 3."* Below: small product mock + "Get my plan."
- **CTA:** *"Get my plan."*
- **Why:** Stat becomes a wedge. Buyer self-identifies into the smart-quartile.
- **Caveat:** verify the stat before shipping. If we can't source it, swap to something we can ("90+ days the average user takes to quit a language app", whatever Baymard or App Annie has).

## 6. The relationship hook
- **Audience:** Partners of native speakers, parents of bilingual kids
- **Hook:** Romantic / familial
- **Visual:** A photo of a family dinner, slightly out of focus. Caption: *"Her mom has never spoken to you in English."* Below, smaller: *"Volley. A real tutor. Daily AI practice. You've got 6 months until the wedding."*
- **CTA:** *"Start with French. Or Italian. Or Mandarin."*
- **Why:** Concrete stakes. Volley becomes a relationship investment, not a self-improvement project.

## 7. Before / after voice memo
- **Audience:** Skeptics who want proof
- **Hook:** Real user transformation
- **Visual:** Two audio waveforms side-by-side. *"Sarah K. · Day 1"* — a halting recording of her introducing herself in French. *"Sarah K. · Day 90"* — same intro, fluent, asking the waiter a follow-up question. (Sarah's testimonial verbatim.) Sound on by default.
- **CTA:** *"This was 3 months. Get my plan."*
- **Why:** Audio is rare in the category. Real-user proof. Specific, named, dated.
- **Caveat:** depends on access to Sarah's actual recordings or willingness to record fresh. If not, this becomes a recreated-with-permission concept.

## 8. The price math (creator / voiceover style)
- **Audience:** Cost-anchored skeptics ("Speak is $20/mo, why is this $99")
- **Hook:** Value reframe
- **Visual:** Person talking to camera, casual: *"$99/month. That's $25 a live lesson with a real tutor. You've been paying $20 to talk to a chatbot. I'm not knocking it. But you stopped opening the chatbot in February."*
- **CTA:** *"Pay for the human. Get the AI for free."*
- **Why:** Owns the price by reframing as a per-unit deal. Implicitly Duo / Speak roast.

## 9. The phone-screen montage
- **Audience:** Multi-app dropouts
- **Hook:** Pattern recognition
- **Visual:** Top-down phone shot. Camera pans across a folder labeled *"Languages."* Inside: Duolingo (last opened: 47 days ago), Babbel (89 days), Pimsleur (153 days), Anki (12 days), Drops (200+ days), Speak (33 days). Cut to Volley app icon, opening to a notification: *"Hey, your tutor noticed you missed Tuesday. Got 15 min for a quick session?"*
- **CTA:** *"Make this the last app on the screen."*
- **Why:** Visual confession. The buyer literally has this folder. No copy needed.

## 10. The slow flex (brand-confident, low-budget)
- **Audience:** Premium-buyer, taste-driven
- **Hook:** Editorial restraint
- **Visual:** Static. Black background. White Fraunces serif: *"You don't need another app. You need a coach."* Below: small line in mono: *"$99/mo. Volley. Real tutors. Real AI. No streaks."*
- **CTA:** *"Get my plan."*
- **Why:** Looks like a New Yorker ad in a feed of Duolingo owls. Buyer notices the silence.

## If we shipped 3, we'd ship these

The challenge brief asks for **3 angles with different audiences, hooks, promises**. The cleanest 3 from the menu:

1. **#3 The tutor introduction** (proof of human · "real coach not chatbot" promise · trust-skeptic audience)
2. **#4 The Duolingo roast** (comedy / counter-position · "noticed when you skip" promise · Duo guilt-pile audience)
3. **#2 The trip countdown** (urgency · "real reason, real timeline" promise · recent-flight-bookers audience)

Why these three: they hit three different funnel stages (top-of-funnel cold awareness via #4, mid-funnel objection-handling via #3, bottom-funnel conversion via #2 retargeting). Different audiences in Meta. Different optimization goals possible (CTR for #4, view-time for #3, leads for #2). They span trust / comedy / urgency without doubling up emotional registers.

**Backup angles if any of the three test soft:**
- #1 (shame thirst-trap) replaces #4 if comedy doesn't land
- #5 (data flex) replaces #3 if proof framing feels too earnest
- #10 (slow flex) replaces #2 for cold-traffic seeding if retargeting pool is too small

---

## What I'd kill first if numbers came in soft

Per Max's "what would you kill first" gate:

- **Kill #4 first** if CPM is high and CTR doesn't compensate. The Duolingo joke depends on Volley's audience also being Duo's audience, which we've assumed but not validated.
- **Kill #2 second** if conversion-to-placement-test stays below 8%. Travel retargeting requires a hot audience; if cold-traffic shows similar lift, #2 isn't earning its targeting overhead.
- **Keep #3 the longest** even at higher CPA. The tutor-introduction creative is the only one that produces a brand asset usable on the page itself, in email onboarding, and in PR. Asset value > ad-set value.

## Notes for the testing plan (separate doc)

The full testing plan (a separate deliverable) should cover:
- Audience definitions (lookalikes, interests, retargeting pools)
- Budget allocation (suggest $1,500/wk split 50/30/20 across the three)
- Optimization goals (Leads / Landing Page View / Reach depending on cohort)
- Statistical significance threshold (95% per the digitalapplied.com study's standard)
- Success metric per angle (placement-test starts is the key in-funnel event)
- Kill criteria per angle (CPL ceiling + minimum CTR)
- Day-by-day decision tree for the 14-day test window
