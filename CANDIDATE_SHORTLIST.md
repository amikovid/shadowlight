## The framework

Three candidates representing **three strategic bets on what the role actually needs.** Not three picks from one search — three hypotheses about where the operator should come from, each represented by the strongest exemplar.

| Bet | Hypothesis | Candidate |
|---|---|---|
| **The Receipts Bet** | Snowball needs a proven multi-million closer who's done the motion at platform scale | **Christian Liquigan** (LA, US) |
| **The Mindshare Bet** | Snowball needs an operator with their own audience who sells the brand publicly while closing privately | **Jack Frimston** (Manchester, UK) |
| **The Native Bet** | Snowball needs vertical fluency over years-of-experience — someone who's been the mechanic, not the manager | **Nate Nkangwen** (Miami, US) |

A fourth bet — **The Rolodex Bet** (day-1 brand-CMO relationships) — was considered and cut. Justin Adelman represented it. Why it got cut, and the full bet-vs-bet decision logic, lives in [research/08_candidate_dossiers.md §6](research/08_candidate_dossiers.md). Short version: **Ryan Hashemi's brand already pulls Fortune 500 CMOs through inbound.** The salesperson's job here is to close what comes in and expand into adjacent verticals — not to bring relationships in their phone. Day-1 relationships are nice; they're not load-bearing for THIS role at THIS stage.

## How I hunted (one line per candidate, for the Loom)

- **Christian** surfaced from a Lens-3 search of senior sales/BD at creator-economy SaaS (Spotter, Captiv8, Jellysmack). The TikTok line is what locked the pick.
- **Jack** surfaced from a Lens-2 sweep of podcast hosts + book authors writing about agency-services sales. The bio voice is what locked the pick.
- **Nate** surfaced from an X behavioral-signal sweep on creator-economy operators publishing public revenue receipts. The Faze Rug client + the $32k thread is what locked the pick.

Three different surfacing methods. Three different "how I found them" stories. None of the three had "Head of Sales at a creator agency" on their CV — exactly what Max asked for.
