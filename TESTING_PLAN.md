## Metrics that matter (different per angle)

Each format has its own leading indicator. Don't read them all on the same dashboard.

| Angle | Lead metric (what tells you it's working) | Lag metric (the conversion) |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Quick Math** | **Save rate >0.4%** (background gag drives rewatch behavior; saves drive shares drive conversions) | CTR >2.5%, CPL <$30 |
| **2. She's a 10** | **Share rate >0.1%** (viral-coded romcom; conversion is brand recall + organic spread) | Comment rate >0.4%, CTR >1.2% acceptable |
| **3. Tutor Manifesto** | **50% completion >55%** (cinematic ads are watched, not skipped) | 75% completion >40%, CTR >1.5%, CPL <$60 |

Across all angles, **placement-test starts** is the in-funnel conversion event. **Active members at day 14** is the lag indicator that matters most.

## Kill order (what dies first if numbers come in soft)

This is the part Max specifically asked about — kill criteria, not just success criteria.

### Kill #1 (first to go): **She's a 10** if shares <0.1% in 5 days

Viral-coded comedy converts indirectly. If shares are flat, the format isn't traveling. Kill paid spend, repurpose into organic — the asset still has brand-voice value, just not at paid CPM.

### Kill #2: **Quick Math** if no variant clears 2.5% CTR after $400 spend

Mid-funnel ads have to convert; if the founder + sight-gag doesn't earn the click at warm-audience level, the angle is structurally wrong (not a creative problem, a thesis problem).

### Kill #3 (last to go): **Tutor Manifesto** stays alive longest

Brand-asset value, longer payback window. **Don't kill before $1,200 spend** regardless of CPL — the 60-second cut becomes the LP hero video and onboarding email content even if it doesn't directly convert.

### What scales

- **Quick Math winner →** 70% of week-2 budget. Variants: failed-proposal background, chicken-suit walk-by, tipping plant variants, different speakers.
- **She's a 10 winner →** Mandarin / Spanish / French language variants, gender-flipped POV, job-interview-stake variant.
- **Tutor Manifesto winner →** Italian / Spanish versions with different senior tutors. Generates the "meet the tutors" series.

## What I'd watch for that this plan doesn't capture

Three known gaps in the test design:

1. **iOS 17 attribution noise.** Meta CPL inflated by 15–30% per iOS reporting changes. Real CPL is lower than what shows in the dashboard. Don't kill on $30 CPL alone — verify with backend placement-test starts.
2. **Weekend-vs-weekday completion bias.** Cinematic ads (Tutor) over-perform on Sat/Sun, retargeting (Quick Math) under-performs. Read 7-day rolling, not daily spikes.
3. **Founder-led content has slower cold ramp.** The Quick Math CEO format may need 3–4 days to find its audience before the algorithm settles. Don't kill at day 2 even if numbers are flat.
