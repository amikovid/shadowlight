# Volley: Product, Presence, and Marketing Recon

What's currently shipping, what's discoverable, and where the gaps are. Every observation here is something we can defend in the Loom.

> **Update note (May 5):** Earlier versions of this doc treated Volley as a pure-AI product because the meta description was the only crawlable text. The live page (getvolley.ai/lp3) is JS-rendered, so fetchers see nothing. Visiting it in a browser revealed the real product is a **hybrid: human tutor + AI practice loop**. This doc has been rewritten against the live page content.

---

## TL;DR

Volley is a real shipped product with a serious distribution problem. **The product is NOT a pure AI app — it's a human tutor paired with daily AI practice.** 4 × 30-minute live tutor lessons per month, plus unlimited in-app AI practice (voice chat, audio lessons, smart flashcards). The tutor reviews your weekly practice data and adjusts your plan. Pricing is gated behind a "Get my plan" CTA + 2-minute placement test. The page itself is competent but conservative — corporate, illustrative, low-confrontation. It does not counter-position. It does not name a category enemy. It does not own an emotional truth. **That's the opening for our variant.**

## What the product actually is

Per the live lp3 hero (verbatim):

> *"Learn a language with a private tutor and daily AI practice."*
> *"4 × 30-minute live lessons with a real tutor each month, plus unlimited AI practice in the app every day."*

Per the role brief (Max's words):

> *"Volley is an AI + human language coaching app at $99/month."*

### Confirmed feature set (from lp3, in the order Volley itself presents them)

1. **A real human tutor** matched to you via 2-minute placement test. 4 × 30-min live lessons/month. Tutor reviews your weekly practice data and adjusts the next week's plan.
2. **AI tutor on demand** — voice chat for free-form practice (order coffee, ask directions, debate politics).
3. **Audio lessons** — 15-minute listen-and-repeat sessions for commutes/walks (the "Lyssna och upprepa" / "listen and repeat" UI is shown in Swedish on the live page, suggesting Swedish is a flagship language).
4. **Smart flashcards** — mistakes in conversation become flashcards you see again the next day. Anki-style "Again / Good / Easy" rating UI.
5. **The weekly loop** — three named steps: (1) Practice in the app, (2) AI tracks every word/mistake/card automatically, (3) Tutor reviews it weekly and adjusts your plan.

### Pricing

- $99/month (per Max's role brief). **Not visible on the public lp3 — gated behind "Get my plan" + placement test.** This is a deliberate lead-capture move.
- No public free trial offer. The funnel is: 2-min placement test → tutor match → first lesson scheduled "this week" → presumably price reveal.
- No public refund/guarantee language on lp3.

## What Volley's current page does well (steal these)

1. **Real testimonials with specific outcomes.** Sarah ordered dinner in French. Anders broke a Duo/Babbel quit-cycle. Marcus had past-tense unlocked. Specific, named, language-attributed. This matches the 2026 LP data on named-customer specificity (+22% lift).
2. **The weekly loop concept is the actual differentiator.** Practice in app → AI tracks → tutor reviews and adjusts. No competitor does this. This is the wedge they should be hitting harder.
3. **"You don't need more content, you need someone making sure you show up"** is the strongest line on the page. It reframes the entire category from "content access" to "accountability." Snowball/Max would approve.
4. **Real product UI screenshots** for each feature (flashcards, voice chat, audio lessons) in actual Swedish — not generic mockups.
5. **Personas are named and pointed** (busy pros, app dropouts, serious learners). Not vague "anyone learning a language."
6. **Tutor-as-a-real-person framing**: actual photo of a tutor, "Your tutor" label, conversational mock between user and tutor. Humanizes the offer.

## What Volley's current page does badly (fix these)

1. **Pricing is hidden.** $99/mo gated behind "Get my plan" → placement test. At a premium price this is a trust hole. Transparent pricing converts better at the high end (Silvi shows the $89 pillowcase price upfront for the same reason).
2. **No counter-positioning.** Page mentions Duo/Babbel/Pimsleur in personas but never names them as enemies. Max's playbook is to name competitors and explain why you're different. The page is too polite.
3. **No risk reversal.** No money-back guarantee, no "first lesson free" promise, no "if your tutor doesn't fit, we re-match" comfort. At $99/mo the buyer needs an out.
4. **Hero is descriptive, not aspirational.** *"Learn a language with a private tutor and daily AI practice"* describes the product. It doesn't sell the outcome or pick a fight. Compare to Snowball's *"We don't follow YouTube best practices. We create them."* — Max writes hero copy that takes a side.
5. **Two CTAs, both "Get my plan."** No CTA hierarchy. No second option for skeptics ("see how it works" or "watch a 60-second demo").
6. **Weak final close.** *"Take a 2-minute placement test. Get matched with a tutor. Start your first lesson this week."* — three steps, no urgency, no payoff sentence.
7. **No founder presence.** Silvi, Snowball, Megaphone all have a named human attached. Volley has none. (We may not be able to fix this on the page itself if the founder isn't public, but it's a noted gap.)
8. **The honest competitor frame is missing.** The page never says "here's what Duolingo can't do." It hints around it. Saying it explicitly is on-brand for Max.

## Competitive context (May 2026)

### The dominant players

**Speak (speak.com)** — *"Talk out loud, get instant feedback, and become fluent."* Pure-AI, ~$20/mo, owns "speaking out loud" as a wedge. **What Volley has that Speak doesn't: a real human tutor reviewing your week.**

**Praktika (praktika.ai)** — *"Private tutor quality, app convenience, truly personal."* Pure-AI but uses the word "tutor." **What Volley has that Praktika doesn't: an actual human, not a tutor-personality bot.**

**Langua (languatalk.com)** — Hybrid AI + human tutor model (started as tutoring, pivoted to AI). #1 in roundup articles. **The closest competitor to Volley structurally.** Volley's differentiator vs Langua is harder to articulate without product-side detail — likely "the weekly review loop is a true product feature, not a bolt-on."

**Loora, Talkpal, Gliglish, Langotalk, TutorLily, Victor AI, Teacher AI, MakesYouFluent** — all pure-AI. None have a human in the loop. **Volley's "real human tutor reviews your week" is unmatched in this set.**

**Duolingo, Babbel, Pimsleur** — the legacy apps Volley's own page calls out by name in the persona section. Volley's frame against them: gamification ≠ accountability.

### The wedge that's actually unclaimed (and matches Volley's real product)

> **"You don't need another app. You need a coach who notices when you skip a day."**

This is the only frame that:
- Matches Volley's actual product (human tutor + AI loop, not just AI)
- Counter-positions cleanly against Speak/Praktika/Langua (pure-AI) AND Duo/Babbel (gamified, no human)
- Lands the strongest line from the existing page ("You need someone making sure you show up")
- Justifies $99/mo (humans cost money; AI alone doesn't)
- Is uncynical and emotionally true (the failure mode of every language app is "I stopped using it")

The earlier recon's "fear of speaking" wedge is real but it's a Speak-shaped wedge. **Volley's real wedge is accountability, not anxiety.**

## What this recon means for the page

1. **The product is the wedge.** Volley's "human tutor + AI loop" is a genuine moat in a category drowning in pure-AI apps. The page should make the human + AI split impossible to miss in the hero. The current page does this competently but quietly. Our version says it louder.

2. **The accountability frame is the unclaimed wedge.** Not "fear of speaking" (Speak-shaped), not "private tutor feel" (Praktika-shaped), not "realistic AI" (Langua-shaped). **"A coach who notices when you skip a day."** This is what their own strongest line already gestures at. We make it the hero.

3. **Pricing should be visible, not gated.** At $99/mo, hiding the price reads as a sales trick. Transparent pricing in the hero (with the full offer breakdown — 4 lessons + AI access + tutor reviews) builds trust at the premium tier. Counter-position: "Yes, $99. Here's what you get for it."

4. **Counter-position three named competitors explicitly.** Per Max's playbook, per Volley's own persona section ("Duolingo, Babbel, Pimsleur"). The page name-drops them but doesn't pick a fight. Our version picks the fight.

5. **Use Volley's real testimonials.** Sarah/Anders/Marcus are named, on-record, language-specific, and outcome-specific. Use them verbatim. Add no fakes.

6. **Add a risk reversal.** Volley currently has none. Even a "first tutor lesson is free if you don't love your match, we re-pair you" is asymmetric trust. (Flag this as a recommendation, not a fabricated promise — the page can describe what we'd add, the deck/Loom explains why.)

7. **The 5-second test:** A first-time visitor must understand within 5 seconds: *what Volley is* (a human tutor + an AI app), *who it's for* (adults who've tried other apps and quit), *why it's different* (the tutor reviews your week and adjusts the plan). The current page takes ~10 seconds to land this because the hero buries the tutor in a subordinate clause. Lead with the tutor.
