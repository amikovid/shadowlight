05 · volley · 3 ad angles

Three angles. Three funnel stages.
Three creative grammars.

Three angles, not five. Find the one winner fast, pour gas. Don't try to maximize coverage, try to compound the win. Praktika went from $1M to $12M ARR in 30 days off one UGC video that hit 32M views. Variant-volume isn't the move. Format-fit-against-funnel-stage is the move.

angle 01 · mid-funnel + viral

Quick Math

format founder direct-to-camera, plant tipping in background length 0:17 cost $0–$100

A founder does $99 price math direct to camera while a tall houseplant slowly tips behind them. Lands at "Do better." Plant falls on cue. Single take. The math is the substance, the gag is the pattern.

Reads as UGC, not creative. Founder hook earns the dunk on the category. Lead metric is save rate, not CTR. Viewers scrub back to confirm what they saw.

angle 02 · top-of-funnel + viral

She's a 10

format POV, motion-pause edits, jazz crescendo length 0:20 cost $200–$500

POV romcom with mid-frame freezes on caption reveals, jazz building under. "She's a 10. Her parents don't speak English. Don't fumble this, king." Volley reveal is brief. Closer is the future mother-in-law squeezing his hand at dinner in Italian.

Format is canonical TikTok, stake is concrete (you can't talk to her parents), payoff is emotional. Judged on shares first, comments second, CTR third.

Kill order, Sunday night.

01She's a 10 dies first if shares stay under 0.1% in 5 days. Comedy converts indirectly. Flat shares mean the format isn't traveling. Repurpose into organic only.
02Quick Math dies second if no variant clears 2.5% CTR after $400 spend. Mid-funnel ads have to convert. If the founder plus sight-gag doesn't earn the click on warm audiences, the angle is structurally wrong.
03Tutor stays alive longest. Brand-asset value, longer payback. Don't kill before $1,200 spend regardless of CPL. The 60-second cut becomes the LP hero video and onboarding email content even if it doesn't directly convert.
if you told me "pick two"

It's Quick Math and She's a 10. The Tutor ad is what week-2 success buys. Highest production cost, longest payback, fund it out of week-1 wins, not as the entry point.

09 · snowball · the cut

A fourth bet was considered and cut.

Justin Adelman represented The Rolodex Bet (day-1 brand-side CMO relationships). He's strong. He didn't make the shortlist. Three reasons, in order.

01

Rolodex is the most-replaceable bet for Snowball specifically. Ryan's 14M followers and ex-Jubilee credibility already pull Fortune 500 CMOs through inbound. The salesperson's job is to close what comes in and expand into adjacent verticals, not to bring relationships in their phone. Day-1 relationships are nice. They're not load-bearing for THIS role at THIS stage.

02

Justin's relationships are partially redundant with Christian's. Both worked the brand-CMO room. Christian also brings the proven ability to build a multi-million book at platform scale. Justin doesn't add a load-bearing capability the other three don't already cover.

03

Justin is the most "obvious-on-paper" of the four. Least off-org-chart. You said: "don't send me obvious org-chart picks." Sending the most-conventional pick of the four against your explicit ask is the one mistake the shortlist couldn't afford.
