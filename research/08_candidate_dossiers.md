## 2. The strategic frame: three bets

The shortlist is not "three candidates from three search lenses." It's **three hypotheses about what Snowball's Head of Sales needs**, each represented by the strongest exemplar.

The four bets we considered:

1. **The Receipts Bet** — *Snowball needs a proven multi-million closer who's done the motion at platform scale.* (Christian)
2. **The Mindshare Bet** — *Snowball needs an operator with their own audience who sells the brand publicly while closing privately.* (Jack)
3. **The Native Bet** — *Snowball needs vertical fluency over years-of-experience. Someone who's been the mechanic, not the manager.* (Nate)
4. **The Rolodex Bet** — *Snowball needs day-1 brand-side CMO relationships, not someone who has to build them.* (Justin — cut, see §6)

**Why the Rolodex Bet got cut:** Snowball's brand (Ryan Hashemi's 14M followers, ex-Jubilee credibility) already pulls Fortune 500 CMOs through inbound. The salesperson's job is to *close* what comes in and *expand* into adjacent verticals — not to open doors that the brand can already open itself. Day-1 relationships are nice; they're not load-bearing for THIS role at THIS stage.

The remaining three bets each address something Ryan/Max cannot do alone:
- They cannot personally carry a $50–500k retainer bag at scale (Receipts)
- They cannot generate independent audience pull from a third operator (Mindshare)
- They are not 23-year-old YouTube-native operators who live the medium daily (Native)

That's the defense. The shortlist hits three load-bearing gaps; the cut bet was the most-replaceable.

## 6. Why we cut Justin Adelman

Justin scored 27/35. He's a real candidate — Group Account Director at Movers+Shakers with a Fortune-500 client roster (e.l.f., Chipotle, L'Oréal, Maserati, Abercrombie, Liberty Mutual). He represented the Rolodex Bet — the hypothesis that Snowball most needs day-1 brand-side CMO relationships.

He didn't make the final shortlist for three reasons:

1. **The Rolodex Bet is the most-replaceable bet for Snowball specifically.** Ryan Hashemi's brand (14M followers, ex-Jubilee) already pulls Fortune 500 CMOs through inbound demand. Snowball doesn't need a salesperson to open doors that the brand can already open itself. What it needs is someone to *close* what comes through and *expand* into adjacent verticals — which is the Receipts Bet's job, not the Rolodex Bet's.
2. **Justin is partially redundant with Christian on brand-side relationships.** Christian has TikTok-era Auto vertical CMOs in his network plus current Spotter relationships. Justin's relationships are sharper and more current, but the overlap means cutting Justin loses less than cutting Christian.
3. **Justin's profile is the most "obvious-on-paper" of the four.** Twelve years at premium agencies, Group AD title, blue-chip roster — defensible but predictable. Max specifically asked for non-org-chart picks. The Combo D shortlist (Christian + Jack + Nate) leans harder into that ask.

**If we'd had a sharper Lens 1 candidate** — someone with hunter-coded language in their profile AND a Fortune-500 roster, instead of an account-director-coded profile — the cut might have gone differently. We didn't surface one, so the Rolodex Bet got cut on principle.

Justin stays on the bench. If any of the three primary candidates declines, he's the next outreach.
