# Volley ad testing plan (1-page exec summary)

> **Short version of the testing approach.** For full creative briefs (scripts, casting, sound, inspirations, A/B variants per angle), see [ADS_CREATIVE_BRIEFS.md](ADS_CREATIVE_BRIEFS.md). For the 10-angle brain-dump that produced these three, see [ADS_BRAINDUMP.md](ADS_BRAINDUMP.md).

## The philosophy

Three angles, not five. Three different funnel stages. Three structurally different creative grammars. **Find the one winner fast, pour gas. Don't try to maximize coverage — try to compound the win.**

Per [research/06_meta_ads_research.md](research/06_meta_ads_research.md), Praktika went from $1M to $12M ARR in 30 days off **one** UGC video that hit 32M views across platforms. The lesson: variant-volume isn't the move. Format-fit-against-funnel-stage is the move.

The three angles map to three funnel stages:

| Angle | Funnel stage | Format | Length | Inspiration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Quick Math** (CEO + plant background gag) | Mid-funnel + viral | Founder direct-to-camera with sight gag | 0:17 | Cliff Weitzman / Speechify TikToks |
| **She's a 10** (POV romcom, motion-pause edits) | Top-of-funnel + viral | TikTok-native vertical POV | 0:20 | TikTok #pov "she's a 10 but" trend, *Past Lives* trailer pacing |
| **Tutor Manifesto** (senior tutor, Sorbonne credentials, Sarah past-tense story) | Cold acquisition / brand-build | Masterclass-format cinematic | 0:39 | Aaron Sorkin Masterclass trailer, NYT Modern Love video pieces |

Three creative grammars, one product. Total runtime: **1:16.** Loom narration: *"Cheap weird one ships first. If it works, scale. The cinematic ad is the brand-builder you fund out of week-1 wins, not the entry point."*

## Lead metrics (different per angle)

Each format has a different leading indicator. Don't read them all on the same dashboard.

| Angle | Lead metric | Lag metric |
|---|---|---|
| **Quick Math** | **Save rate** >0.4% (background gag drives rewatches → saves → shares → conversions) | CTR >2.5%, CPL <$30 |
| **She's a 10** | **Share rate** >0.1% (viral comedy converts indirectly) | Comments >0.4%, CTR >1.2% |
| **Tutor Manifesto** | **50% completion** >55% (cinematic ads are watched, not skipped) | 75% completion >40%, CTR >1.5%, CPL <$60 |

**In-funnel conversion event**: placement-test starts. **Lag indicator that matters most**: active members at day 14.

---

## Kill order (Sunday night, end of week 1)

1. **She's a 10 dies first** if shares <0.1% in 5 days. Comedy/viral converts indirectly; flat shares = the format isn't traveling. Repurpose into organic only.
2. **Quick Math dies second** if no variant clears 2.5% CTR after $400 spend. Mid-funnel ads have to convert; if the founder + sight-gag doesn't earn the click on warm audiences, the angle is structurally wrong.
3. **Tutor Manifesto stays alive longest.** Brand-asset value, longer payback. Don't kill before $1,200 spend regardless of CPL — the 60-second cut becomes the LP hero video and onboarding email content even if it doesn't directly convert.

## What I'd watch for that this plan doesn't capture

Three known gaps:

1. **iOS 17 attribution noise.** Meta CPL is inflated 15–30% per iOS reporting changes. Don't kill on dashboard CPL alone — verify with backend placement-test starts.
2. **Weekend-vs-weekday completion bias.** Cinematic ads (Tutor) over-perform Sat/Sun, retargeting (Quick Math) under-performs. Read 7-day rolling, not daily spikes.
3. **Founder-led cold ramp.** Quick Math may need 3–4 days to find its audience before the Andromeda algorithm settles. Don't kill at day 2 even if numbers are flat.

---

## What I'd kill from THIS PLAN if forced to ship two of three

If budget pressure or production timeline forces a cut: **drop Angle 3 (Tutor Manifesto).** Highest production cost ($500–$1,500). Quick Math + She's a 10 cover mid-funnel + viral on $0–$400 production budgets. Tutor is the brand-asset luxury — fund it out of week-1 wins, not as the entry point.

The Loom narration for that call: *"If you told me 'pick two,' it's Quick Math and She's a 10. The Tutor ad is what week-2 success buys."*
