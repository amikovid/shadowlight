# Snowball Head of Sales — recruitment gameplan

> Working reference doc for the Snowball recruiting deliverable. Use this while hunting. Has the full search frame, paste-ready boolean strings, niche behavioral-signal sources, fit rubric, and outreach scaffolds. The deliverable per [research/04_challenge_brief.md](04_challenge_brief.md) is **3 candidates with LinkedIn link, role/company, 2–3 sentences why, and outreach copy.** Max's note: *"Don't send me obvious org-chart picks. Show me how you hunt."*

---

## 1. The role frame

**Snowball Agency** (snowball.agency) — Max's YouTube creative agency, built around Ryan Hashemi (ex-Jubilee Media, 14M+ followers, 5B+ views). They make YouTube content for brands. Sells **premium services** to brand-side buyers — CMOs, Heads of Brand, VP Marketing.

**Sales motion (inferred):** $50k–$500k/month retainers. Long sales cycles (1–4 months). Custom scope per client. ICP is consumer brands wanting creator-led growth, founder-led video presence, or YouTube channel build-out.

**What this Head of Sales has to do:**
- Personally close $X+ in retainers (player-coach, on the line)
- Talk shop with brand-side marketers about content + creator economy fluently
- Calibrate scope and pricing live in calls with CMOs
- Build a small sales team over time, but Year 1 they're carrying the bag

**What this Head of Sales is NOT:**
- A SaaS-style AE running a fixed playbook
- A management-of-managers role
- A pure "VP Sales" who delegates everything

### Comp + geo (per Kovid, May 2026)

- **Comp:** under $200k OTE total. Likely structured as $130–150k base + 25–35% commission. **Note: this is below market for most SaaS Head of Sales — but on-market for agency Director/Head roles, especially smaller boutique shops. Filter affordability accordingly.**
- **Geo:** truly global. AU/US/UK/EU/CA all in. Remote-first assumed.

### What Max grades on (per [04_challenge_brief.md](04_challenge_brief.md))

> *"Don't send me obvious org-chart picks. Show me how you hunt — who you found, why them, and what would make them actually open your DM."*

Three implicit grading criteria:
1. **Heterogeneity** — three candidates from three different paths to the role, not three Head-of-Sales-at-peer-agencies.
2. **Hunting story** — the *how* matters. The deck should narrate why these three were surfaced from where.
3. **Outreach quality** — would Max himself open these DMs? Would the candidate?

## 2. The three search lenses (run in parallel)

### Lens 1 — "On-the-line operator" (the half-org-chart pick)

Has personally closed $1M+ in services. At a small-to-mid agency or creator-economy company. Tenure ripe for a move. NOT the obvious peer-agency Head of Sales.

**Why this lens isn't org-chart:** the obvious org-chart pick is "Head of Sales at Jubilee/Whalar/Studio71" — too senior, too expensive, won't accept $200k. The interesting Lens 1 pick is **a Director-level operator at an agency just below the obvious tier**, ripe for a Head-of role.

### Lens 2 — "Has the creator-economy mindshare" (the off-org-chart pick)

Publishes about creator economy / agency growth / founder-led content. Has an audience. **They've never been "Head of Sales" but they've been selling the same thing all along** — to their own audience or as an operator. Twitter / LinkedIn / Substack / Beehiiv writers in this space.

**Why this lens beats org-chart:** the people who can EXPLAIN the creator economy in public are usually the ones who can SELL it in private. And they're often more reachable and more excited about the role than a tenured Head of Sales.

### Lens 3 — "Adjacent jumper" (the lateral pick)

Senior sales/BD at a creator-economy SaaS or premium podcast/media company. Not at an agency now, but selling premium services to the same buyer (CMO at a consumer brand). Would jump into agency-side for the upside and the proximity to Ryan/Max.

**Why this lens beats org-chart:** they already speak the language of the buyer. The motion (premium services to brand-side) translates directly. They're often UNDER-comped at the SaaS company because they're sales-3 or sales-4, not the Head — making the Snowball role a step up *and* a comp upgrade.

## 4. The niche behavioral angles (Kovid's request)

These are the signals that surface candidates *org-chart searches miss*. Run AFTER you have a baseline list from Lenses 1–3, OR run in parallel for true off-org-chart picks.

### A. Specific phrases and verbal tics

People who SELL agency services well USE specific language. The phrases below show up in posts/profiles when someone is operator-class. Use them as keyword filters in LinkedIn / Apollo / Twitter:

- *"agency math"* — speaks the unit economics language
- *"first sales hire"* — built it from zero, knows what early-stage sales feels like
- *"player-coach"* — self-aware about being on the line, not above it
- *"closed $X this month"* — tracks numbers publicly, brag about it
- *"founder-led"* / *"founder-led content"* — knows the medium
- *"services pricing"* / *"retainer pricing"* — has actually done the negotiation
- *"creator-led"* / *"creator-first"*
- *"$X ARR"* — operator language
- *"60-day pipeline"* / *"90-day pipeline"* — talks pipeline shape, signals ops maturity
- *"channel growth"* in YouTube context (not "channel partner" SaaS context)

**Tool:** LinkedIn search for posts containing these phrases in the last 90 days, sorted by engagement. Twitter advanced search same.

### B. Online referrals / shoutouts

Operators who are GOOD at this often get shouted out by other operators. Search for:

- LinkedIn / Twitter posts that contain *"the best at"* + *"creator"* OR *"agency sales"* OR *"YouTube"*
- *"closed $X for us"* — when a brand-side person credits a salesperson by name
- Twitter threads: *"who would you hire as Head of Sales"* type posts and answers
- Hampton-internal / Trends.co / Operators-only Slack referral threads (if Kovid has access)
- Y Combinator's Hacker News *"Who's Hiring / Who Wants to Be Hired"* threads — sometimes operators self-post

**Tool:** Twitter search:
```
"who should I hire" OR "who's the best at" "agency" OR "creator" OR "sales" min_faves:10 lang:en
```

### C. Deep-engagement readers, not creators

This is the under-discussed signal. People who *engage* (like, comment, repost) on creator-economy content for 2+ years are operators-in-training, even if they've never posted themselves. They're the underpriced segment.

**How to find them:**
- Look at the *commenters* on Lia Haberman's / Justin Welsh's / Sahil Bloom's creator-economy posts. Filter to the ones whose comments are SUBSTANTIVE (cite numbers, share their own experience, disagree with specifics). Those are operators.
- Look at people who repost Snowball's or Jubilee's content and add commentary.
- Look at people in the comments of Mr Beast / Colin and Samir / Jubilee YouTube videos — their bio says "agency sales," "creator manager," etc.

**Tool:** there's no clean tool for this. It's manual scraping. 30 minutes on the right post yields 5–10 candidates.

### D. Side projects / personal Substacks / Beehiivs

People sourcing themselves in public. If they run their own paid newsletter on creator economy AND have a sales/BD job, they're a Lens 2 candidate. Look at:

- Substack writers in the "Marketing" / "Business" categories with <5,000 subscribers (under-discovered)
- Beehiiv writers tagged "creator-economy" or "agency" with <2,500 subscribers
- LinkedIn profile bios that link to a personal newsletter

**Tool:** Substack discover + Beehiiv explore.

### E. Workshop / coaching cross-pollinators

Operators sometimes monetize their expertise via workshops or coaching, in addition to a day job. The combination signals: they know the work AND they can teach it (= can sell it). Look at:

- Maven course instructors in marketing / agency / creator categories
- Reforge facilitators in the same
- People running "agency growth" cohorts on Circle / Mighty Networks

**Tool:** Maven (maven.com), Reforge (reforge.com), Circle (circle.so).

### F. Specific date / event triggers

People in transition are reachable. Look for:

- Layoff announcements — search Twitter for *"laid off"* + *"creator economy"* OR *"agency"* in the last 60 days. Tragic but true.
- Company shutdowns — recent creator-agency deaths (e.g. Whalar / Fullscreen restructurings) leave operators looking. News search.
- M&A — recent creator-agency acquisitions create displaced senior salespeople.

**Tool:** TheLayoff, Layoffs.fyi (filter Marketing/Agency), Twitter advanced search, Crunchbase notifications.

### G. Open-to-Work proxy

Don't filter for *"#OpenToWork"* directly (low-quality bias). Instead filter for:

- Title change in last 60 days
- *"Recently moved on"* / *"Next chapter"* in profile or last post
- New "Independent" / "Consulting" / "Advisor" status next to a sales background — often a 6-month layover before they want a real role again

**Tool:** Sales Nav "Recently changed jobs" filter.

## 5. Fit rubric (score each candidate 1–5 per dimension)

Use this scoresheet to triage every candidate that surfaces. **Threshold for shortlist: 22+ out of 35.**

| # | Dimension | What 5/5 looks like | What 1/5 looks like | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Sales motion fit** | Has personally closed $1M+ in services/retainer contracts | Pure SaaS quota carrier, no services experience | 1.5x |
| 2 | **Creator-economy fluency** | Uses the right vocabulary in public posts. Has worked with creators directly. | Says "influencer marketing" with no nuance. Has never shipped a creator-led campaign. | 1.5x |
| 3 | **Network strength** | Has direct relationships with brand-side CMOs / Heads of Brand at consumer brands | Pure inside-agency network, no buyer-side relationships | 1x |
| 4 | **Operator energy (player-coach)** | Specific evidence of being hands-on (built first pipeline, ran first campaigns themselves) | "Manager of managers" type, hasn't carried a bag in 3+ years | 1x |
| 5 | **Storytelling / sales chops** | Can articulate the creator-led value prop in a 90-second elevator pitch you'd want to hear | Generic sales person, no point of view | 1x |
| 6 | **Cultural fit (under-the-radar, non-LinkedIn-influencer-coded)** | Posts substantively, not for clout. Doesn't say "thought leader" in bio. | LinkedIn influencer with motivational quotes and "DM me to learn how I 10x'd ARR" | 0.5x |
| 7 | **Comp realism** | Currently at $130–180k OTE band. Open to lateral / step-up move. | $300k+ comp at a holdco, won't take the offer | 1x |

**Total: 35 weighted points.** Below 22 = pass. 22–28 = "decent." 28+ = strong shortlist candidate.

The three final picks should ideally:
- Each score 28+ on the rubric
- Come from three DIFFERENT lenses (1, 2, 3)
- Have heterogeneous backgrounds (not three white men in their 40s from peer agencies — looks like a copy-paste shortlist)

## 6. Outreach scaffolds

Three outreach templates, calibrated to which lens surfaced the candidate. **Voice: Max-coded** (they're getting an email that purports to be from Max or his team). Direct, no fluff, named-specific.

### Template A — Lens 1 (On-the-line operator)

> Subject: *"$X retainers at [their company] — quick ask"*
>
> Hey [first name],
>
> Saw you've been running sales at [their company] for [X years]. From what I can tell from outside, the [specific deal / specific brand] partnership is the kind of motion we're trying to scale here at Snowball.
>
> I'm working with Max Hertan and Ryan Hashemi (snowball.agency, ex-Jubilee Media). We're looking for a Head of Sales who's actually carried a bag — not a holdco veteran, not a generalist VP. Someone who's closed services deals with brand-side CMOs and wants to do that at scale with creator-led work.
>
> 200k OTE, global remote. Comp lower than Big Agency, equity higher. Mostly we hire on whether we'd want to be in the trenches with you.
>
> Worth a 20-min call this or next week?
>
> [signoff]

### Template B — Lens 2 (Mindshare operator)

> Subject: *"Your post on [specific topic] — Snowball is looking"*
>
> Hey [first name],
>
> Read your [post / newsletter / podcast] on [specific topic — e.g. "agency math after the influencer downturn"]. The point about [specific argument they made] is what we're betting our pipeline on at Snowball this year.
>
> I'm working with Max Hertan and Ryan Hashemi (snowball.agency, ex-Jubilee Media — 14M followers, 5B views). We're hiring a Head of Sales. The role is half-and-half — half closing brand-side retainers, half helping us figure out where the next 18 months of creator-led work goes. Player-coach, no holdco politics.
>
> Comp's under 200 OTE, but the upside (equity, strategic seat) is worth the conversation if you've never run a P&L this directly before.
>
> Worth 20 min?
>
> [signoff]

### Template C — Lens 3 (Adjacent jumper)

> Subject: *"Senior at [their SaaS company] — agency-side opening"*
>
> Hey [first name],
>
> [Specific compliment on a public win — e.g. "saw your name on the Beehiiv x [brand] case study"]. The kind of brand-side selling you're already doing at [their company] is structurally identical to what we're building at Snowball.
>
> We're a YouTube creative agency (Max Hertan + Ryan Hashemi, ex-Jubilee Media). Looking for a Head of Sales — someone who already speaks the brand-side language but wants the agency-services upside instead of platform comp ceilings.
>
> 200k OTE, global remote, equity meaningful. The pitch: you keep the buyer relationship muscle, swap the "platform" comp story for "operator" comp story.
>
> 20 min this week?
>
> [signoff]

### Why these work

- **Subject lines are specific** — names a deal, a post, or a SaaS company. Not "great opportunity" generic.
- **First sentence is a deposit** — names something specific the candidate did. Earns the next 30 seconds of attention.
- **Names Ryan + Max + the credentials** — the receipts (14M followers, 5B views) are the mic drop.
- **Comp is honest upfront** — under $200k OTE. The right candidate self-selects; the wrong one filters out fast.
- **Asks for 20 min, not "a quick chat"** — concrete > vague.

### What to NEVER do

- Don't open with "I came across your profile" (low-quality signal, instant DM-detector trigger)
- Don't say "exciting opportunity" (LinkedIn-spam coded)
- Don't lead with the role title; lead with their work
- Don't hide the comp range until call 2
- Don't write more than 5 sentences. The right candidate will reply to a 5-sentence email; the wrong one needs convincing.

## 8. The "show me how you hunt" Loom narration outline

Per Max's challenge brief, the Loom is where this is won or lost. Three beats to narrate when walking through the Snowball block:

1. **The frame:** *"I built three search lenses — on-the-line operator, mindshare operator, adjacent jumper. Then I layered behavioral signals on top — specific phrases people use, podcast guest lists, online shoutouts, conference speakers."*

2. **The picks:** for each of the three candidates, narrate which lens surfaced them and the specific signal that made them stand out. *"Candidate 1 came from the operator lens — but what flagged her was a Twitter post in March where she explained the agency math behind a $250k retainer. That's not a person reading from a script."*

3. **The outreach:** *"For each, I wrote the outreach to the specific signal that surfaced them. The deal she bragged about. The newsletter post he wrote. The SaaS case study that's structurally identical to what Snowball would do."*

That's the three-beat: *what I built, who it surfaced, why they'd open the DM.*

## 10. Quick-reference: what NOT to do

- ❌ Don't shortlist any current Head-of-Sales at Jubilee, MrBeast, Whalar, Studio71 — too obvious, too senior, too expensive.
- ❌ Don't shortlist anyone with 5+ years of pure SaaS career.
- ❌ Don't shortlist anyone whose LinkedIn bio includes "thought leader," "AI evangelist," or motivational quote.
- ❌ Don't shortlist anyone who hasn't posted in 6+ months — out of touch with the medium.

- ❌ Don't pretend you found someone "by chance" — the deck should NARRATE the search method.
- ❌ Don't write the outreach until the candidate is locked. Different candidates need different first sentences.

## 11. Open questions I'd ask Max if he were here

These don't block the work but would sharpen it. Note them for the Loom — *"things I'd ask in the first call":*

1. **What's the current sales structure at Snowball?** Is the Head of Sales replacing someone, or building from zero? Massively changes who fits.
2. **What's the average deal size today?** $50k retainer vs $500k retainer = different sales motion = different person.
3. **What's the sales-to-CEO ratio of effort?** If Ryan/Max are still on every pitch, the Head of Sales is a different role than if they're handing off entirely.
4. **What's the geography of the buyer base?** US-heavy → US hire. AU-heavy → AU hire. Global → genuinely flexible.

If we shipped the Loom asking these out loud, that's *also* a signal of how a Chief of Staff would actually think about the role.
